Verizon · Experience Architecture · 2026–Present

Verizon Identity
Architecture

The invisible structure behind connected lives at scale.

Most people never see this work. That's the point. Leading Verizon's identity and profile ecosystem, creating the infrastructure that connects households, devices, behaviors, and services into one coherent, intelligent system.

Role: Principal Product Designer · Timeline: 2026–Present · Status: Active
Core Diagnosis

How did we get here?

Verizon's identity ecosystem grew through acquisition and parallel product development, not design. Dozens of product lines, separate account models, and inconsistent data definitions left customers navigating a fragmented experience, and teams building on unstable ground.

Core diagnosis — fragmentation across product lines, account models, and data definitions
The Challenge

Why this is so hard.

When the surface area spans consumer, small business, enterprise, and IoT, coherent decision-making requires shared infrastructure. Every team is doing the right thing locally. The system still fails globally. And the consequences of doing nothing compound quietly, year over year.

Why this is uniquely hard at Verizon's scale
What's at risk if fragmentation continues
Strategic Vision

From fragmentation to a
unified identity core.

The work begins with shared language. Defining what identity means across every product surface, and building the architecture that allows individual teams to operate independently while contributing to a coherent whole. Less integration tax. More compound value over time.

Current state to strategic vision — the architecture transition model
Execution

Sequence over speed.

A phased model that respects organizational reality. The first 30 days establish vocabulary and identify the highest-leverage integration points. The next 60 validate the model against live products. By day 90, the architecture is de-risked, documented, and adopted by the first wave of product teams.

30–60–90 execution plan — phased adoption designed around organizational constraints
Business & Customer Impact

Infrastructure that earns its keep.

When identity architecture works, it disappears. Customers move between Verizon products without friction. Teams build faster on a shared foundation. And the business gains the intelligence layer needed to deliver genuinely personalized experiences at scale, across every product and surface.

Business and customer impact — how coherent identity creates compounding value across the ecosystem
