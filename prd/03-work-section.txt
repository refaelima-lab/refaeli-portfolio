Case Study Introduction Section Specification
Purpose
This section acts as a transition moment before entering the detailed case studies.
It should provide:
* a visual and emotional reset
* breathing room after the Experience and Principles sections
* a shift from philosophy into proof
This section should feel:
* calm
* cinematic
* spacious
* intentional
* immersive
The goal is to slow the pacing slightly before introducing the featured work.
________________
Section Title
MADE REAL
This title should feel:
* grounded
* confident
* understated
Typography should carry strong presence through:
* spacing
* scale
* rhythm
Avoid decorative treatments.
________________
Supporting Copy
A selection of recent work in digital product and interaction design, focused on clarity, systems, and intelligent experiences.
Tone:
* precise
* restrained
* mature
* quietly confident
Avoid:
* marketing language
* exaggerated claims
* excessive explanation
________________
Visual Direction
This section should remain intentionally minimal.
It should function as:
a cinematic pause between chapters.
The atmosphere should feel:
* elegant
* architectural
* ambient
* slightly futuristic
* immersive without distraction
________________
Background Animation Requirements
Use the same visual language introduced in the Hero section.
Potential elements:
* rotating line systems
* geometric structures
* moving dots
* orbital motion
* connected nodes
* flowing line patterns
* subtle particle movement
Visual language should feel:
* systematic
* intelligent
* lightweight
* atmospheric
Avoid:
* heavy sci-fi visuals
* flashy particle systems
* chaotic movement
* excessive visual density
________________
Motion Requirements
Motion should feel:
* slow
* ambient
* continuous
* meditative
Potential motion behaviors:
* rotating geometric systems
* drifting connection lines
* orbital dot movement
* layered parallax depth
* soft opacity transitions
Animation should support atmosphere rather than demand attention.
________________
Layout Requirements
The composition should remain:
* centered or asymmetrically balanced
* spacious
* typography-forward
Allow generous breathing room around:
* title
* supporting copy
* animated background elements
The section should visually prepare the user for deeper exploration.
________________
Transition Requirements
This section should transition smoothly into the featured case studies.
Potential ideas:
* background system gradually becoming more structured
* lines evolving into case study layout grid
* geometric movement subtly guiding toward first project
* opacity shift into project reveal
The transition should feel:
seamless and intentional.
________________
Emotional Goal
The viewer should feel:
“Now I’m entering the proof behind the philosophy.”
