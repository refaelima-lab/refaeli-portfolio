Timeline Content
2011–2013
Finding Product Thinking
The beginning of my journey started in product management, where I first became fascinated by how ideas evolve into real experiences.
This was the moment I realized I wasn’t only interested in making things look better, I was deeply interested in how products work, how decisions are made, and how systems shape behavior.
That curiosity eventually led me toward design.
________________


2013–2018
ArtCenter College of Design
Industrial & Product Design
(Add ArtCenter logo)
Some of the most formative years of my life.
I studied, interned, experimented, failed, rebuilt, and learned what it truly means to care deeply about craft and ideas. These years shaped both my discipline and my ambition as a designer.
During this time, I also attended a short study program at IDA International Design Academy in Italy, where I explored design through a more global and cultural perspective.
In 2016, I had the opportunity to collaborate with the Amazon Echo team on future vision concepts for the Echo ecosystem.
(Add Amazon / Echo visual treatment)
My design  work later received recognition through:
* European Product Design Award
* A’ Design Award
* Bienenstock Furniture Library
I was also honored to exhibit work at the 29th Annual ICFF NYC show.
(Add award logos)
Later, I became a Jury Member for the A’ Design Award.
Between 2016–2017, I was invited back to ArtCenter as a guest professor for the Product Design studio class, a surreal and meaningful full-circle moment.
________________


2018–2021
Building Perspective
The first major step after graduation was founding my own design studio:
13 Orphans
(Add 13 Orphans logo)
13 Orphans became my creative anchor, a place to freely explore ideas, experiment across disciplines, and define my own relationship with design beyond school or industry expectations.
Separate from 13 orphans studio - During these years, I worked across agencies, startups, and fast-moving client environments.
I collaborated with teams including:
* Phenomenon Product Design
* Unicorns & Unicorns
These experiences taught me:
* how to communicate ideas clearly
* how to balance craft with timelines
* how to manage multiple complex projects
* how to present and defend design decisions
* how to adapt design thinking across industries and business models
Separate from 13 orphans studio - During these years, Working within startups also pushed me closer to the full product lifecycle.
I learned the importance of asking difficult questions early, understanding product strategy, and thinking beyond the interface itself.
This was the period where I stopped seeing designers as isolated creators and started seeing design as part of a larger business and operational ecosystem.
________________


In-Car Experience Design
In 2021, I entered the automotive and in-car experience industry.
(Add automotive brand logos/icons:
BMW, KIA, Ford, Porsche)
This became one of the most transformative chapters of my career.
It allowed me to combine:
* industrial design
* digital product design
* systems thinking
* physical interaction design
Working across transportation ecosystems taught me that digital experiences never exist in isolation. Every interface lives inside a physical environment shaped by motion, context, accessibility, culture, and hardware constraints.
One of the most meaningful experiences during this time was collaborating with the KIA team in Korea to help develop a scalable design system spanning multiple screen sizes and vehicle environments.
This work reshaped how I think about:
* accessibility
* responsive systems
* transportation UX
* physical-to-digital interaction
* ecosystem consistency
It was also the period where I began leading and mentoring more designers.
________________


2021–2023
BlueJeans by Verizon
(Add BlueJeans logo)
Joined BlueJeans by Verizon as Principal Designer during one of the most defining moments in modern digital communication.
I led work across the Remote Learning platform during the pandemic, helping support millions of users navigating a rapidly changing world of remote interaction and education.
This became the second-largest design system I had built at the time:
* 900+ components
* scalable cross-platform infrastructure
* enterprise-level consistency
(CTA → View Case Study)
This chapter fundamentally reshaped how I think about digital behavior, communication, and the emotional role products play during moments of global uncertainty.
It made me realize we were entering a new era of human-computer interaction, where digital products were no longer supporting life in the background, they were becoming part of how people learn, connect, and exist daily.
________________


Mentorship & Leadership
Around this time, I also became a business mentor at Institute for Entrepreneurial Leadership and design career coach at the 4A’s Foundation..
(add IFEL and 4As logo)
After receiving so much from this industry, I wanted to give something back.
Working with founders, business owners, and emerging designers helped me better understand leadership, communication, and the responsibility of helping others grow.
This experience played a major role in shaping who I became as a design manager and mentor.

2023–Present
